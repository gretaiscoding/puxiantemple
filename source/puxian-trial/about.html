How It Started

The smallest Buddhist temple in the world was founded in August 1975 , known as the Gem of the Buddha Temple ( Fo Pow Meow) and later being renamed to Puxian Temple for Samantabhadra Vihara.

Many years back, the founding leader of Pu Xian Temple formerly know as gem of the Buddha Temple (FO POW MEOW), Bro. Tan Kooi Beng led the youngest independent, non-sectarian Buddhist organisation in Malaysia. He started to spread Buddhism at his age of 15 to some local boys and girls in his village. By collecting TEN cents each from them, he managed to gather a total of twenty-seven Malaysian ringgit as an activity fund.

Pu Xian Temple, a young and independent organization, faced financial challenges in its early days. They raised 1,200 Malaysian Ringgit by selling bookmarks, enough to build only four pillars. To fund their expansion, members took on odd jobs, provided boating services, and ran bicycle parks, contributing all earnings to the project. It took four months to complete a four-foot-high wall due to financial constraints.
After a year of struggling, generous donations from philanthropist Datuk Low Hooi Siah made the temple's expansion possible. Today, it proudly stands in the Jelutong district. The young leaders of Pu Xian Temple, despite minimal public help, showed determination and resilience in their efforts, making it the world's smallest Buddhist Temple and the youngest registered Buddhist organization in Malaysia.
